Workspace Collaboration Glossary

A compact noun set for designing shared workspaces, teams, departments, groups, and access inside a SaaS app.

Recommended default: use Organization for the customer/company boundary, Workspace for a shared product home, Team for a durable human working group, Group for access or notification membership, and Role for permissions.

Core Terms

Organization

The customer or company container.

Usually the billing, compliance, identity, and administration boundary. GitHub organizations and Slack Enterprise organizations both use this idea for managing many people and shared work.

Tenant

The internal isolation boundary.

Often an engineering synonym for organization. It is useful in schemas and infrastructure, but usually too technical for user-facing navigation.

Workspace

A shared product home.

A workspace is where members collaborate. Linear describes a workspace as the container for company work concepts; Figma uses workspaces to group related teams and members inside an organization.

Team

A durable group of people who work together.

Teams are usually visible and human-readable: Engineering, Design, Support. Linear and GitHub both use teams to reflect working groups and access patterns.

Department

An org-chart function.

A department is a kind of team when you mirror the company chart: Engineering, Sales, Finance. Use it only if the product cares about the formal org chart.

Group

A membership set for access, mentions, or provisioning.

Groups are more mechanical than teams. Slack user groups have names, handles, members, and default channels; GitHub teams can also function as access and mention groups.

Role

A permission level at a scope.

Owner, admin, member, guest, viewer, editor, and maintainer are roles. A role says what someone can do; it does not say where they belong socially.

Membership

The join record between a person and a container.

A membership connects a user to an organization, workspace, team, group, or resource. It often carries role, state, invite source, and timestamps.

Resource

The thing people work on or access.

Examples: channel, project, document, issue, file, board, database, or thread. Resources may belong to a workspace or team and can have their own permissions.

Space

A content or collaboration area.

Use space when the object is more about content location than people. Notion's teamspace is a good example: a dedicated area for a team with its own access mode.

Useful Distinctions

Distinction Use this when... Avoid this mistake
Organization vs workspace The organization owns billing, identity, and policy; the workspace is where work happens. Do not expose both if your product has only one meaningful shared home per company.
Team vs group A team is human and durable; a group is a membership mechanism for access, mentions, or provisioning. Do not call every permission list a team.
Department vs team A department follows the formal org chart; a team may be cross-functional or temporary. Do not force cross-functional work into a department tree.
Role vs group A role defines capabilities; a group collects people. Do not create groups named admins if the real concept is an admin role.
Resource vs container A channel, page, project, or file is something people access inside a wider container. Do not use workspace for every shareable object.

Starter Model

For an app that lets companies share work, start with this conceptual model before adding complexity:

  • Organization owns billing, policies, identity, and top-level administration.
  • Workspace is the shared home where day-to-day app resources live.
  • User joins through Membership records.
  • Team represents visible working groups inside a workspace or organization.
  • Group represents access, notification, or provisioning sets.
  • Role is assigned at a scope: organization, workspace, team, group, or resource.
