The ownership question

Every Bap resource should answer these four questions before you expose it in a team product.

Question Example answer
Who can see it? Support team, Revenue team, or only Baptiste.
Who can change it? Workspace admins and coworker owners.
Whose account acts? Shared support Gmail, Alice's Slack, or a service account.
Who approves risk? Specific users, an approver group, or the requester.

Suggested First Product Cut

Build first Why User-facing name
Workspace switcher Gives personal and company work a clear boundary. Workspace
Members and invitations Lets a company share Bap before adding hierarchy. Members
Teams Maps resources to human working groups. Teams
Coworkers Makes the AI actor explicit: purpose, tools, memory, workflows, approvals. Coworkers
Groups Handles approvers and access without confusing them with teams. Groups

Primary Source to Revisit

Read Linear's Teams documentation again with one Bap-specific question in mind: where Linear says "team owns work," Bap may need "coworker acts for a team."

Related Reference

Use the Workspace Collaboration Glossary when deciding whether a Bap concept should be named organization, workspace, team, group, role, coworker, or resource. Ask the agent follow-up questions when the model starts feeling ambiguous; ambiguity here usually means the permission boundary is not explicit yet.
