# Workspace and Team Management Resources

## Knowledge

- [Linear Docs: Concepts](https://linear.app/docs/conceptual-model)
  Linear's concise model for workspace, teams, issues, projects, and initiatives. Use for: how a work-management product separates the company container from team-level work.
- [Linear Docs: Teams](https://linear.app/docs/teams)
  Practical guidance on when to split a workspace into teams, including the advice to start with one or two teams. Use for: avoiding premature team hierarchy.
- [Notion Help: Intro to teamspaces](https://www.notion.com/help/intro-to-teamspaces)
  Notion's user-facing model for dedicated content areas owned by teams. Use for: naming content spaces that map to teams and access rules.
- [Notion Guide: Teamspaces for admins](https://www.notion.com/help/guides/what-are-teamspaces-a-guide-for-notion-admins)
  Admin-facing framing for teamspace access modes and security settings. Use for: open, closed, and private collaboration areas.
- [Slack Developer Docs: Enterprise organizations](https://docs.slack.dev/enterprise/)
  Slack's distinction between Enterprise organization, workspace, shared channel, and user identity. Use for: multi-workspace and enterprise vocabulary.
- [Slack Help: What is a channel?](https://slack.com/help/articles/360017938993-What-is-a-channel)
  Slack's conversation-space model and public/private visibility split. Use for: separating people containers from conversation resources.
- [Slack Help: Create and edit user groups](https://slack.com/help/articles/212906697-Create-and-edit-user-groups)
  Slack's model for user groups with names, handles, members, and default channels. Use for: permission/notification groups.
- [Google Workspace Directory API: Organizational units](https://developers.google.com/workspace/admin/directory/v1/guides/manage-org-units)
  Google's hierarchical organizational unit model with inheritance. Use for: understanding department-like trees versus many-to-many groups.
- [GitHub Docs: About organization teams](https://docs.github.com/en/organizations/organizing-members-into-teams/about-teams)
  GitHub's model for teams as organization member groups with access, mentions, visibility, and nesting. Use for: teams as access and notification units.
- [Figma: Workspaces in Figma Enterprise](https://www.figma.com/best-practices/workspaces-in-figma-enterprise/)
  Figma's enterprise model where workspaces group related teams and members within an organization. Use for: workspace as navigation and delegated administration layer.
- [Figma Help: Team permissions](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039970673-Team-permissions)
  Figma's permissions for teams, including view/edit access for organization or workspace members. Use for: understanding how a collaborative canvas separates team membership from file/project access.
- [Figma Help: Guide to sharing and permissions](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/1500007609322-Guide-to-sharing-and-permissions)
  Figma's resource hierarchy for organizations, teams, projects, files, and prototypes. Use for: understanding inherited versus granular resource sharing.
- [Figma Help: Share files and prototypes](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040531773-Share-files-and-prototypes)
  Figma's file share modal, including invited people, public access, organization/workspace access, prototype-only links, passwords, link expiration, and search visibility. Use for: designing share dialogs with multiple audiences.
- [Figma Help: Guide to files and projects](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/1500005554982-Guide-to-files-and-projects)
  Figma's distinction between personal drafts and team projects. Use for: private authoring spaces before team sharing.
- [Figma Help: Manage public link sharing and open sessions](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/5726756336791-Manage-public-link-sharing-and-open-sessions)
  Figma's organization controls for public links, passwords, expiration, and disabling public sharing. Use for: enterprise policy over otherwise easy collaboration.
- [Lovable Docs: Control project access](https://docs.lovable.dev/features/project-visibility)
  Lovable's current project access model, including Workspace and Restricted access, public remixing, and the separation between project access and published website access. Use for: separating coworker editor access from public outputs.
- [Lovable Docs: Share a project](https://docs.lovable.dev/features/share-project)
  Lovable's share dialog, explicit collaborators, workspace groups, external collaborators, roles, expiring invite links, and preview links. Use for: modeling direct grants, group grants, and public view-only previews.
- [Lovable Docs: Workspace](https://docs.lovable.dev/features/workspace)
  Lovable's workspace as the container for projects, billing, credits, members, and security settings. Use for: deciding what should belong to a Bap workspace.
- [Lovable Docs: Workspace and project knowledge](https://docs.lovable.dev/features/knowledge)
  Lovable's split between workspace-wide persistent instructions and project-specific knowledge. Use for: thinking about shared coworker context versus per-coworker context.
- [Miro Help: Sharing boards and inviting collaborators](https://help.miro.com/hc/en-us/articles/360017730813-Sharing-boards-and-inviting-collaborators)
  Miro's board sharing model, including team-level access, guest links, and view/comment/edit permissions. Use for: studying resource-level collaboration around a shared canvas.
- [Miro Help: Team permissions on Enterprise](https://help.miro.com/hc/en-us/articles/4402822899986-Team-permissions-on-Enterprise-plan)
  Miro's enterprise team discovery, invitation, sharing, and board-content settings. Use for: team-level policy controls.
- [Miro Help: Public boards vs. private boards](https://help.miro.com/hc/en-us/articles/360011114519-Public-boards-vs-private-boards)
  Miro's private-board definition and plan-dependent defaults. Use for: avoiding ambiguous "private" language.
- [Miro Help: Who has access to my board?](https://help.miro.com/hc/en-us/articles/4408888726290-Who-has-access-to-my-board)
  Miro's explanation that boards can be shared at several levels at once and the highest access applies. Use for: access-resolution rules.
- [Miro Help: Visitors, guests and members](https://help.miro.com/hc/en-us/articles/7045408248594-Visitors-guests-and-members)
  Miro's distinction between anonymous link visitors, registered board guests, and full team members. Use for: external sharing without adding someone to the workspace.
- [Asana Help: Team basics](https://help.asana.com/s/article/team-basics)
  Asana's team model for grouping people and projects. Use for: how a work-management app exposes human teams.
- [Asana Help: Project permissions](https://help.asana.com/s/article/project-permissions)
  Asana's project-level permission model. Use for: separating project access from team membership.
- [Airtable Support: Permissions overview](https://support.airtable.com/docs/airtable-permissions-overview)
  Airtable's permissions across workspaces, bases, interfaces, and admin panel. Use for: studying layered permissions over structured data.
- [Airtable Support: Managing and sharing interfaces](https://support.airtable.com/docs/managing-and-sharing-interfaces)
  Airtable's model for sharing user-facing interfaces on top of underlying bases. Use for: thinking about Bap views that expose limited capabilities without exposing all underlying data.
- [Google Drive Help: Share files](https://support.google.com/drive/answer/2494822)
  Google's familiar file sharing model: restricted or link access, with viewer/commenter/editor roles. Use for: clear resource-level permission language.
- [Google Workspace Learning Center: Create a shared drive](https://support.google.com/a/users/answer/9310249)
  Google's shared drive membership and role model. Use for: organization-owned resources instead of user-owned files.
- [Microsoft Learn: Shared channels in Teams](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoftteams/shared-channels)
  Microsoft Teams' shared-channel model for collaborating with people outside the team. Use for: cross-boundary collaboration without inviting someone into everything.
- [Dropbox Help: Team folders overview](https://help.dropbox.com/organize/team-folders)
  Dropbox's team folder model. Use for: understanding team-owned shared content.
- [Dropbox Developers: Team files guide](https://developers.dropbox.com/dbx-team-files-guide)
  Dropbox's recommendation to assign team-folder permissions through groups. Use for: why access groups scale better than direct per-person grants.
- [GitLab Docs: Groups](https://docs.gitlab.com/user/group/)
  GitLab's groups as containers for related projects, members, communication, and inherited permissions. Use for: nested group/project access patterns.
- [GitLab Docs: Roles and permissions](https://docs.gitlab.com/user/permissions/)
  GitLab's role assignment model for groups and projects. Use for: scoped roles in engineering collaboration.
