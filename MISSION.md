# Mission: Workspace and Team Management Vocabulary

## Why
Baptiste wants to design user grouping inside the app with clearer product language. The immediate goal is to understand how collaboration products name shared spaces, departments, teams, groups, and permissions so the app can model groups of users without borrowing confused terminology.

## Success looks like
- Explain the difference between an organization, workspace, team, group, department, role, and resource.
- Choose user-facing names that fit the app's actual sharing and permission model.
- Sketch a simple membership model for a company with departments, cross-functional teams, guests, and admins.
- Recognize when a noun should be user-facing product language versus internal implementation language.

## Constraints
- Keep lessons short and directly tied to product design decisions in this app.
- Prefer primary documentation from established collaboration tools over generic SaaS advice.
- Build a reusable glossary before moving into schema or permissions design.

## Out of scope
- Implementing the data model in code.
- Enterprise identity provider details beyond the vocabulary needed to design the app.
